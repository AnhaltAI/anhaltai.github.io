abstract: 'In a highly competitive market such as the automotive sector it is no longer sufficient to define quality solely on technical aspects.
Rather, quality must be analyzed holistically from the perspective of the customer. The customer perception of a product decides the buying
behavior. Therefore, Kano et al. have created a special survey form to
distinguish between different quality categories for product features. It
allows a cost-optimized product design, maximizing customer satisfaction. Practical usage has shown that applying a Kano survey is a very
critical process. In this work an alternative data source is proposed that
bypasses systematically the problems of a Kano survey: the Internet.
Due to the increasing number of fora, weblogs and other social networks
there is a large amount of unsolicitedly provided and mostly unbiased
customer feedback available. This is used in a prototype architecture to
obtain customer satisfaction for different product features and the corresponding influence on customer perception. Different abstraction levels
provide information to improve this perception over time.'
